About Breast Cancer Staging

Cancer staging is a critical step in the diagnostic and treatment planning process. The AJCC (American Joint Committee on Cancer) TNM staging system is the most widely used classification, evaluating tumor size (T), lymph node involvement (N), and distant metastasis (M).

In breast cancer, accurate staging helps determine prognosis, guides therapy decisions (surgery, chemotherapy, radiation), and allows comparison of outcomes across clinical studies. This staging system is often applied following imaging procedures such as FDG PET-CT (Fluorodeoxyglucose Positron Emission Tomography – Computed Tomography), MRI, and ultrasound to assess the full extent of disease involvement.

FDG PET-CT plays a vital role in advanced breast cancer cases where distant metastasis is suspected. It provides functional imaging that complements anatomical findings, making it valuable in M staging (metastasis). The combination of clinical evaluation, imaging, biopsy, and pathological data ensures accurate TNM assignment and subsequent staging.

Understanding and applying the TNM system allows clinicians to offer personalized treatment strategies and monitor disease progression or recurrence over time.
